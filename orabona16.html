Open Problem: Parameter-Free and Scale-Free Online Algorithms

Francesco Orabona, Dávid Pál
29th Annual Conference on Learning Theory, pp. 1659–1664, 2016

Abstract

Existing vanilla algorithms for online linear optimization have \(O((\eta R(u) + 1/\eta) \sqrt{T})\) regret with respect to any competitor \(u\), where \(R(u)\) is a \(1\)-strongly convex regularizer and \(\eta > 0\) is a tuning parameter of the algorithm. For certain decision sets and regularizers, the so-called parameter-free algorithms have \(\widetilde O(\sqrt{R(u) T})\) regret with respect to any competitor \(u\). Vanilla algorithm can achieve the same bound only for a fixed competitor \(u\) known ahead of time by setting \(\eta = 1/\sqrt{R(u)}\). A drawback of both vanilla and parameter-free algorithms is that they assume that the norm of the loss vectors is bounded by a constant known to the algorithm. There exist scale-free algorithms that have \(O((\eta R(u) + 1/\eta) \sqrt{T} \max_{1 \le t \le T} \norm{\ell_t})\) regret with respect to any competitor \(u\) and for any sequence of loss vector \(\ell_1, \dots, \ell_T\). Parameter-free analogue of scale-free algorithms have never been designed. Is is possible to design algorithms that are simultaneously parameter-free and scale-free?
