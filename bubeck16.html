Multi-scale exploration of convex functions and bandit convex optimization

Sébastien Bubeck, Ronen Eldan
29th Annual Conference on Learning Theory, pp. 583–589, 2016

Abstract

We construct a new map from a convex function to a distribution on its domain, with the property that this distribution is a multi-scale exploration of the function. We use this map to solve a decade-old open problem in adversarial bandit convex optimization by showing that the minimax regret for this problem is \(\tilde{O}(\mathrm{poly}(n) \sqrt{T})\), where \(n\) is the dimension and \(T\) the number of rounds. This bound is obtained by studying the dual Bayesian maximin regret via the information ratio analysis of Russo and Van Roy, and then using the multi-scale exploration to construct a new algorithm for the Bayesian convex bandit problem.
